### Open Models have crossed a threshold
*[Source: rss | 2026-04-02]*

This LangChain blog post highlights how open models like GLM-5 and MiniMax M2.7 have reached parity with closed models in core agent capabilities, including file operations, tool use, and instruction following, based on their internal evaluations that measure success rates and latency. What's novel is the emphasis on real-world applicability, with detailed benchmarks showing these models handle function calls more efficiently at a fraction of the cost, making them viable for production-scale testing. For Connectly's Talk Framework, this is directly relevant as we could swap in these models for our simulated personas to accelerate multi-turn tests and tool-use evaluations—potentially cutting costs by 50-70% while maintaining accuracy, but we'd have to run our own regressions to ensure they don't introduce issues like inconsistent API calls in diverse languages or ambiguous queries.
